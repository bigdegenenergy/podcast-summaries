  culminating in the drafting of the prospectus.


  The core narrative then pivots to how **AI can intervene proactively**. AI tools
  can anticipate potential regulatory, operational, and structural issues *during*
  the term sheet creation phase by understanding the known concerns of various stakeholders.
  This allows firms to structure interactions more efficiently, generate a stronger
  initial term sheet, and even use AI to draft preliminary prospectus versions based
  on historical structures. The ultimate goal is to significantly reduce external
  legal fees and internal business costs associated with personnel time.


  A key challenge discussed is the dependency of this advanced AI application on having
  **experience/knowledge** about stakeholder concerns, rather than requiring a fully
  populated "single source of truth" database upfront. The AI uses this knowledge
  base to propose structures and run scenarios (e.g., testing monthly redemptions
  against investment policy constraints) before the final data structure is locked
  down.


  Finally, the discussion emphasizes the necessity of **human oversight**. While AI
  can automate screening of changing regulations and extract key information from
  white papers, the outputs—especially those related to legal or compliance documents—must
  be thoroughly checked by human professionals before finalization to ensure regulatory
  validity and maintain investor trust.

  concerns to generate initial, robust proposals, effectively creating a "single source
  of truth" framework early on.

  3.  **Cost Reduction via Efficiency:** By streamlining the term sheet creation and
  anticipating issues, AI directly reduces external legal fees and the internal business
