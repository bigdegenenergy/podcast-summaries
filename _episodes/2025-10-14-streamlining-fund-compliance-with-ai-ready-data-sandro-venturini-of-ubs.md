  - Sandro Venturini of UBS


  This episode focuses on the foundational challenges in cross-border fund launches—specifically
  the fragmentation of data across legal, operations, and distribution teams—and how
  AI can be leveraged to create efficiency, reduce costs, and establish a "single
  source of truth" before regulatory filings begin.


  ---


  ### 1. Focus Area

  The primary focus is the **application of AI/ML (specifically predictive and generative
  capabilities)** to **streamline complex, cross-border fund structuring and compliance
  processes**. The discussion centers on moving away from siloed spreadsheets to an
  AI-informed system that anticipates conflicts between fund terms (e.g., investment
  policy vs. liquidity terms) early in the process.


  ### 2. Key Technical Insights

  *   **Anticipatory Issue Identification:** AI can analyze proposed fund terms (like
  fees, liquidity, and investment policy) against historical data and stakeholder
  concerns to proactively flag potential regulatory, operational, or structural issues
  *before* they manifest during prospectus drafting or regulatory filing.

  *   **Term Sheet Optimization:** AI tools can help generate a robust, conflict-free
  term sheet by modeling scenarios (e.g., the impact of monthly redemptions on cash
  levels) and structuring stakeholder interactions more efficiently.

  *   **Regulatory Monitoring Automation:** AI is currently capable of automating
  the manual lift of screening regulatory updates, white papers, and extracting relevant
  information to keep compliance teams informed on changing cross-border requirements.


  ### 3. Business/Investment Angle

  *   **Significant Cost Reduction:** Streamlining the formation process by reducing
  first-pass legal errors and minimizing the time spent by internal subject matter
  experts (SMEs) directly translates to lower external legal fees and reduced internal
  business costs.

  *   **Mitigating Business Risk:** Fragmented data leads to reactive firefighting,
  which erodes investor trust and increases regulatory exposure. AI-driven alignment
  creates a more solid, compliant product foundation faster.

  *   **Focus Shift for Professionals:** As AI handles more manual data synthesis
  and initial drafting, financial professionals will shift toward higher-level oversight,
  validation, and strategic interpretation of AI outputs, rather than manual data
  compilation.

  application of these concepts within a major global financial firm.

  *   **Stakeholders Mentioned:** Legal management, distribution, operations, finance,
  tax, compliance, sub-administrators, transfer agents, and auditors—all parties whose
  input must be synthesized.


  ### 5. Future Implications

  The industry is moving toward a model where **AI acts as a foundational layer**
  that synthesizes complex, multi-stakeholder requirements into a coherent structure
  *before* the creation of the official "golden source" database. The future emphasizes
  **human oversight and validation** as the critical safeguard, ensuring that AI-generated
  legal and compliance drafts are thoroughly checked by external counsel and internal
  experts before deployment.
